Quick Tips for a Killer Resume

Tailor Every Time

Customize your resume for each job. Mirror keywords from the job description to pass ATS filters.

Quantify Impact

Use numbers and metrics. "Increased sales by 25%" is far more powerful than "helped improve sales."

Proofread Twice

Typos and grammar errors are instant deal-breakers. Always have someone else review your resume.

One Page Rule

Keep it to one page if you have under 10 years of experience. Recruiters spend ~7 seconds per resume.

Action Verbs First

Start each bullet with a strong verb: "Led," "Built," "Optimized," "Launched" — not "Responsible for."

Clean Formatting

Use consistent fonts, clear headings, and enough white space. A cluttered resume gets skipped.

Pro Tips & Tricks

Use Action Verbs

Start bullet points with strong action verbs like "Spearheaded," "Developed," or "Optimized" instead of "Responsible for."

Quantify Achievements

Numbers speak louder than words. Mention percentages, dollar amounts, or team sizes (e.g., "Increased sales by 15%").

Tailor Keywords

Borrow exact keywords from the job description. ATS systems scan for these to rank your resume.

Keep it Concise

Stick to 1 page if you have less than 10 years of experience. Be ruthlessly brief, keeping only relevant information.

Showcase Soft Skills

Don't just list "Leadership." Give an example: "Led a cross-functional team of 5 to deliver the project ahead of schedule."
